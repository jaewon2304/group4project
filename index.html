DESCRIPTION

How CO2 emissions is considered in each perspective of science?

Chemistry

  • CO2 production is formed by “traditional” fuel.

Biology

  • CO₂ levels in atmosphere have increased over past 250 years, and has been attributed to increasing mean global temperature during this time. With environmental destruction of organisms that use CO₂ in metabolism, the total flux also increased.
  • Methane is at a much lower concentration in the atmosphere in relation to CO₂, but Methane has absorption properties (infra-red radiation) that make it considerably more dangerous than Carbon Dioxide. Produced largely by cows and methanogenic archaeans in bogs, but will be oxidized eventually into CO₂, hence low levels.
  • CO₂ release can cause Carbon Dioxide to dissolve into the oceans, causing a 30% acidification in the water, lowering pH. Dissolved CO₂ decreases concentration of carbonate ions in seawater (essentail for coral skeletal building), meaning new calcium carbonate cannot be forms, but also existing corals reliant on CaCO₃ are at risk of dissolution. This water also continues out into other plants through the water cycle, possibly damaging photosynthetic processes of land plants that are sensitive to pH.

ESS

  • If a pH is too high or too low for a certain body of water, the result would be that the organisms within the body of water would die out. The pH within a body of water, can also affect the solubility and toxicity of heavy metals within the particular body of water. The overall effect on a certain body of water, condoned through the distributions of species would be vast.
    Different species prefer different pH levels, so if the pH would change up somehow-it would create an imbalance in the distribution of them.
  • Methane is very hazardous towards the environment, it is a primary contributor to ground-level ozone. This is a very hazardous air pollutant and a very powerful greenhouse gas Methane would cause a lot of premature deaths because of its pure toxicity.
    Methane primarily leaves the environment through oxidation and the formation of water vapor and carbon dioxide. Leading it to be one of the greatest effects on global warming, it works as a sort of thermal blanket. It slows down the rate at which heat would leave the planet, causing more heat to sustain in the earth’s atmosphere, causing more global warming.

Experiment

Meterials: three same plants / 50ml sylinder / 110ml of pH-1, pH-7 and pH-12 solution

  1. Get three same plants and mark them as 'pH-1', 'pH-7' and 'pH-12' each.
  2. Get three different cylinders, mark them as 'pH-1', 'pH-7' and 'pH-12' each.
  3. Pour 20ml of pH-1, pH-7 and ph-12 solution to three cylinders.
    (Use appropriate cylinders for each solution.)
  4. Water the plant. (Each plant is watered with proper solution.)
  5. Water the plants in this way for 5 days.
  6. After 5 days, check the difference between each plant.
pH-1 plant
Acid Solution
pH-7 plant
Neutral Solution
pH-12 plant
Basic Solution

Result

What is the influences of CO2 Emissions to us?

  • pH-1 plant

    The plant watered with pH-1 solution has changed. Firstly, the whole plant became more yellowish than before. Secondly, the tip of the leaves is dried. This implies that acid water has a negative impact on plants. The plant doesn't stand strongly.

  • pH-7 plant

    The plant watered with normal water (pH-7) wasn't changed at all. Superficially, every part is the same. Rather, the plant became more fresh than before.

  • pH-12 plant

    The plant watered with pH-12 solution also wasn't changed. the pH-12 plant got the similar result to the pH-7 plant.

  • Sum up

    Only the plant watered with acid water is changed. The pH-1 plant cannot stand strongly also. It shows that the acidic water gives a negative effect to plants. While plants with other solutions don't have significant differences.
